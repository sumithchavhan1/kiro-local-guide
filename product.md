# Local Guide Context - Mumbai

## City Profile
Mumbai is the financial capital of India, known for its diverse culture, iconic landmarks, and rich street food culture.

## Local Slang & Expressions

### Common Mumbai Slang
- **Bambai**: Local term for Mumbai
- **Tapri**: Small roadside eatery or tea stall
- **Bhav**: Price or cost
- **Masti**: Fun or mischief
- **Chhakka**: A turn or intersection
- **Bandh**: Strike or closure
- **Mazaa**: Enjoyment or fun
- **Phataka**: A big event or explosion

## Street Food Specialties

### Must-Try Street Foods
- **Vada Pav**: Fried potato dumpling in bread, "Mumbai's burger"
- **Pav Bhaji**: Spiced vegetable curry with buttered bread
- **Bhel Puri**: Puffed rice snack mix with tamarind chutney
- **Chaat**: Savory snack category including golgappe, sev puri
- **Misal Pav**: Spicy curry with bread from Western India
- **Dahi Puri**: Fried puri with yogurt and chutneys
- **Pani Puri / Golgappe**: Hollow puri filled with spiced water

### Famous Street Food Vendors
- **Chowpatty Beach**: Popular beach snacking destination
- **Mohammed Ali Road**: Famous for evening food market
- **Fort Area**: Upscale street food

## Traffic & Transportation

### Peak Traffic Times
- **Morning Rush**: 7:00 AM - 10:00 AM
- **Evening Rush**: 5:00 PM - 9:00 PM
- **Monsoon**: June-September (increased congestion)

### Local Shortcuts
- **Eastern Freeway**: Bypass to North Mumbai
- **SCLR**: Southern Coastal Connector Road for Marine Lines
- **Peddar Road**: Best avoided during peak hours

### Public Transport
- **Local Trains**: Lifeline of Mumbai, always crowded
- **BEST Buses**: Major public transport
- **Auto-rickshaws**: Not metered, negotiate before boarding
- **Taxis**: Yellow cabs, use meters

## Local Weather Patterns
- **Winter**: Dec-Feb, Pleasant (20-32°C)
- **Monsoon**: Jun-Sep, Heavy rainfall
- **Summer**: Mar-May, Hot & humid (30-40°C)

## Cultural Events & Festivals
- **Ganesh Chaturthi**: September festival
- **Diwali**: Festival of lights (Oct-Nov)
- **Navratri**: 9-day festival in Sept-Oct
- **New Year Celebrations**: Marine Drive becomes pedestrian zone

## Shopping Areas
- **Bandra**: Hip shopping and dining
- **Andheri**: Bollywood area
- **Dadar**: Traditional shopping
- **Fort/South Mumbai**: Premium brands

## Tourist Attractions
- **Gateway of India**: Iconic monument
- **Marine Drive**: Coastal promenade
- **Colaba Causeway**: Shopping street
- **Dharavi**: Asia's largest slum (tours available)
- **Elephant Caves**: Historical rock-cut caves

## Important Local Knowledge
- **Best time to visit**: October-February
- **Local language**: Marathi, Hindi, English widely spoken
- **Payment**: Cash and cards accepted, UPI popular
- **Safety**: Generally safe, avoid deserted areas at night
